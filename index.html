CORVID-19

19 (mostly true and interesting) facts about the Corvidae family

Number 1

The family Corvidae includes Crows, Ravens, Jays, Pies, Nutcrackers, and Choughs. Crows happen to be my favorite, so buckle up.

Number 2

They have been described as the smartest birds on Earth

Number 3

Crows have regional dialects. Have you ever been minding your own business while on vacation only to wake up to a bunch of crows with southern accents screaming outside of your window? I have.

Number 4

Crows can use tools to get food. One time a local murder conned me into giving them an entire large order of fries from McDonald's.

Number 5

Crows can recognize human faces, can hold grudges, and can equip weapons (see Number 3).

Number 6

Proportionally, some crow's brains are bigger than yours. (You know who you are..oh wait, you probably don't).

Number 7

Crows can be found on every continent except Antarctica - so there's not many places left to hide.

Number 8

Crows do in fact have 2 eyes.

Number 9

Crows will harass predators much larger than them, and will steal their food. They are the honey badgers of the sky.

Number 10

Crows can learn to mimic human speech

Number 11

Crows mate for life. (Mostly) When it comes to mating, some crows will mate with other crows outside their pairing.

Number 12

Crows host funerals for their dead. When a crow dies, the other crows gather around the remains. Some researchers suggest that they may not be mourning, but may be examing the body and surrounding area for potential threats to the rest of the flock.

Lucky Number 13

Various East Asian mythologies feature 3-legged crows. They are believed to inhabit and represent the Sun.

Number 14

Crows have around 250 different calls and are actually classified as songbirds.

Number 15

Crows have a lifespan of around 20 years, with captive crows living longer than their wild counterparts. The oldest recorded captive crow lived to the age of 59.

Number 16

Crows have caused blackouts. In Japan crows build their nests on power lines and transformers, causing short circuits that lead to blackouts. The power companies now provide fake nests to discourage nest building.

Number 17

"Crow's Nest" - Some etymologists believe that crows or ravens were kept in cages on board ships. They would be released from the top of the mast and would fly directly towards land, thus showing the way.

Number 18

Not all crows have black feathers. Leucistic crows commonly have a few white feathers and albino crows lack all pigment and have pink eyes.

...and finally Number 19!

We're shifting this one back to the Raven side of the corvidae family, and arguably my favourite characters in mythology. Huginn & Muninn! From Norse mythology Huginn (old norse for "thought") and Muninn (old norse for "memory" or "mind") are a pair of ravens that fly all over the world and bring back information to Odin.
